TreeTrip is a CO2e calculator that helps people understand the environmental impacts of their travel. Try it on Heroku.

Project: TreeTrip

Team: Miriam Boran, Alasdair Carstairs

Role: Digital Product Design / Front End Development

Technologies: NodeJS, Webpack, HTML and CSS

Timeline: One week in May 2019

Context: Treetrip was a one week group project at Codeclan to develop a carbon footprint calculator web application in NodeJS.

Having reviewed several existing carbon footprint calculators, we discovered that transportation was our largest source of carbon impact, and indeed is the largest source of carbon impact for most individuals in the UK.

We decided to create a simple calculator based on the UK government carbon emissions conversion factors where users can determine the carbon footprint of their travel based on an short questionaire.

UX Challenges

The biggest challenge of the project was creating an intuitive user experience for the complex branching structure of the form. We wanted it to be dynamically generated, so that the app could be updated with new transportation methods (such as space travel) over time.

This proved to be doubly difficult because the UK government resource for carbon emission factors of different transportation methods was an excel spreadsheet. To ensure our form worked seamlessly, we had to build our own API in a structured logic tree using MongoDB.

UX Solutions

By presenting each question individually on a full-width page, the complexity of the decision tree was hidden inside a simple, linear series of inputs. Once the user enters their information, they receive a colourful display of the scale of their carbon impact using three separate metrics - CO2e, trees required and social cost.

While the social cost and CO2e metrics are fairly standard across most carbon footprint calculators, the trees metric, derived from the average number of trees required to offset the user's travel, provides the user with a powerful visualization of their carbon footprint. Users are also presented with a chart compairing their mode of transport against the number of trees required for alternative travel options.
