Individual Project at Codeclan

Role: Product Designer and Ruby Developer

Brief: Create a full-stack ruby app in a one week sprint.

Technologies: Vanilla Ruby, PostgreSQL, Sinatra, HTML and CSS

Timeline: April 2019

I wanted to create a tool that could be used by fellow Codeclan students to help them during their job search.

Research Findings

In order to better understand my user base, I conducted a short google survey. It helped to answer a few of my questions about people’s job seeking habits

  • 26% of respondants appled for upwards of 15+ jobs during their last job search
  • More than half of respondants use digital tools to track their job search; however, only 5% schedule their meetings and follow ups in a calendar.
  • The majority of respondants did not feel that their current methods helped them feel organized.

To help my job seeking process, I need...

  • ‘analytics on successes and failures’
  • ‘automatically generated cover letters because I hate writing them’
  • ‘suggestions of jobs that are relevant to me’
  • ‘motivational reminders to not procrastinate’
  • ‘to start keeping everything in one place’

UX Challenges

Technology was a challenging constraint while designing the user experience of the application. The brief required a functioning prototype developed using only using only Vanilla Ruby; planning an app without Javascript meant that new information has to be restricted to new page loads.

UX Solutions

Two primary views were developed where most of the necessary information could be found and edited, which helped to keep page loads to a minimum.

  • Dashboard View displays all the leads and their current status. Leads can be created and sorted by company, upcoming deadlines or application status.
  • Detail View shows all the information specific to a lead, including company profiles, job location, and upcoming action items such as interview dates and application deadlines. From the detailed view, users are also able to edit, and delete leads, and update their lead status with new tasks, such as sending a follow up email, or scheduling an interview date.

Visual Design

The app adopts a minimal, information focused layout with hard edges and clean type. Colour were used spairingly and only to draw attention to clickable elements and key information.

Group Project at Codeclan

Team: Miriam Boran, Alasdair Carstairs

Role: Product Designer (UX Design, Overall design language), Developer (form logic)

Brief: Create a carbon footprint calculator in a one week sprint.

Technologies: NodeJS, Webpack, Express, MongoDB, Nodemon, HTML and CSS

Timeline: May 2019

Having reviewed several existing carbon footprint calculators, we discovered that transportation was our largest source of carbon impact, and indeed is the largest source of carbon impact for most individuals in the UK.

We decided to create a simple calculator based off the UK government carbon emissions conversion factors where users can determine the carbon footprint of their travel based on an short questionaire.

UX Challenges

The biggest challenge of the project was creating an intuitive user experience for the complex branching structure of the form. We wanted it to be dynamically generated, so that the app could be updated with new transportation methods (such as space travel) over time.

This proved to be doubly challenging because the UK government resource for carbon emission factors of different transportation methods was an excell spreadsheet. To ensure our calculator worked seamlessly, we had to build our own API using MongoDB.
